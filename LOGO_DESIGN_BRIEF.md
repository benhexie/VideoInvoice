# VideoInvoice — Logo Design Brief

## App Overview

**VideoInvoice** is a quoting and invoicing app for iOS and Android built for tradespeople and contractors. The core experience: record a short video or type a description of a job, and get a professional, itemized quote instantly. Supports price list uploads and polished PDF invoice exports.

**Target users:** Contractors, builders, plumbers, electricians, landscapers, freelancers — anyone who needs to turn a site walkthrough into a professional quote in under a minute.

**Brand positioning:** Fast, intelligent, professional. Not a generic invoicing tool — a smarter way for tradespeople to quote so they can stay focused on the work.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

These are the exact brand colors from the app — the logo must align with them.

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary accent** | `#4F46E5` | Main CTA buttons, active states — the brand color |
| **Accent light** | `#818CF8` | Highlights, glow effects |
| **Dark background** | `#09090B` | Near-black — primary dark mode bg |
| **Light background** | `#FAFAFA` | Off-white — primary light mode bg |
| **Success green** | `#10B981` | Confirmed states |
| **Surface dark** | `#18181B` | Cards on dark bg |

The logo must look great on **both** `#09090B` (dark) and `#FAFAFA` (light) backgrounds. Provide separate lockups for each.

### Typography Direction

- The wordmark **"VideoInvoice"** should feel **modern and geometric** — think rounded sans-serif (similar to Inter, Satoshi, or Geist).
- Treat it as a single bold wordmark. A subtle color split between "Video" and "Invoice" is welcome if it reads cleanly.
- No scripts, no serifs, no vintage styles.

### Icon / Symbol Concept

The mark should capture the core tension of the product: **video in, invoice out**. That transformation — raw footage becoming a polished financial document — is the idea worth visualizing.

**Directions to explore:**

1. **The transform mark** — An abstract shape that reads as both a play button and a document simultaneously. Not two things side by side — one unified form that suggests both.
2. **The frame** — A camera viewfinder or film frame whose inner negative space resolves into a receipt or line-item list. The "looking through" metaphor.
3. **The fold** — A geometric shape that appears to be folding or cutting from one state into another. Evokes conversion, process, output — without being literal.
4. **Lettermark** — A custom "V" or "VI" monogram where the geometry is pulled from the app's grid — sharp angles meeting a document baseline.

No lightning bolts. No zap icons. Nothing that reads as "generic tech startup."

The icon should be **bold enough to read at 20px** (tab bar) and **striking at 1024px** (app store). Avoid thin lines that disappear at small sizes.

## Tone & Style

**Do:** Clean, modern, confident, minimal, tech-forward, slightly bold.

**Don't:** Cartoonish, overly corporate/enterprise, handcrafted/rustic, gradients that look like 2015 SaaS, clipart document icons, literal camera + paper combos.

**References / mood:**
- Linear, Vercel, Raycast (clean dark-mode-first product design)
- Stripe (professional, trustworthy, modern financial tool)
- Notion's simplicity, but with more energy — there's urgency here

---

## App Icon Specs (iOS / Android)

The app icon is the most important part of this brief. It will be cropped to a **rounded rectangle (iOS)** or **circle (Android)**.

- **Background:** Solid `#4F46E5` (indigo) or `#09090B` (near black) — your call
- **Icon mark:** White or `#818CF8` symbol, centered, occupying ~60% of the canvas
- Should feel at home next to Linear, Stripe, and Notion on a phone home screen
- No text in the app icon — symbol only

---

## What to Avoid

- Generic invoice/receipt clip art
- Lightning bolts or electric/zap imagery
- Rainbow or multi-gradient color schemes — the palette is intentionally restrained
- Drop shadows or heavy bevels
- Thin strokes below 2px at any intended display size

## Questions for the Designer

1. Should the icon lean into the **video/camera** metaphor (the input) or the **invoice/document** metaphor (the output) — or try to hold both?
2. Preference on icon background: solid indigo `#4F46E5` or near-black `#09090B`?
